This file is the single editorial source for potential portfolio entries. It is
intentionally more complete than the website should be. We can refine claims,
roles, dates, links, organizational ownership, current status, and emphasis

## Editorial recommendation

### Recommended information architecture

Keep the home page selective. Feature three career-defining products in
reverse chronological order:

1. **SRS Registry** — serves as the culmination and featured case study,
   demonstrating national-scale impact, partner management, and sustained
   collaboration across clinical societies, technology teams, and
   participating centers.
2. **Quentry** — expands the story into secure cloud collaboration, structured
   healthcare data, and multidisciplinary product leadership.
3. **VoyantFlow** — establishes clinical workflow and medical-imaging product
   experience.

Add a **View all work** link after these entries. A future Work page can contain
the full set, grouped as:

- **Professional foundation:** OfficePACS and CasePlan at Stryker—two separate
  large-scale clinical technology deployments.
- **Career-defining work:** VoyantFlow, Quentry, and SRS Registry.
- **Current explorations:** Pathways, VirtusTrack, and Quentry Sync.

This split is preferable to placing all six full entries on the home page. The
current page is already a long-form introduction; six equally weighted projects
would dilute the strongest evidence of professional impact. The independent
projects remain useful, but they support a different message: what Joel is
building and learning now.

### Recommended home-page hierarchy

- Give VoyantFlow the shortest treatment.
- Give Quentry a medium treatment and link its name to <https://www.quentry.com/>.
- Give SRS Registry approximately twice the visual space of either earlier
  entry and label it **Featured work**.
- Present SRS Registry as the result of a career progression, not simply the
  third item in a list.
- Keep technology tags secondary. Lead with the problem, people, scale, and
  outcome.

### Trademark and attribution policy

Use trademarks for factual identification only. The portfolio should describe
Joel's work without implying that he owns the products, currently represents
the organizations, or has their endorsement.

#### Usage rules

- Preserve each product and organization name's official capitalization.
- Use a trademark symbol only on the first prominent occurrence of a marked
  name on a page. Subsequent references should omit the symbol for readability.
- Use **VoyantFlow™** on first prominent occurrence. Historical public product
  materials use the `™` designation.
- Use **Stryker®**, **TraumaCad®**, **Quentry®**, and **Brainlab®** on first
  prominent occurrence. Brainlab's published trademark guidance covers
  TraumaCad, Quentry, and Brainlab; Stryker's published guidance covers Stryker.
- Use **OfficePACS** without a trademark symbol unless historical Stryker brand
  guidance or product documentation confirms a preferred designation.
- Use **CasePlan** without a trademark symbol. The historical U.S. registration
  was active during Joel's tenure but was later cancelled; the current
  portfolio should not display a registered-trademark symbol.
- If the platform-specific SRS product name is used, write **Quentry for SRS®**
  exactly. Do not substitute that name for the broader **SRS Registry** unless
  the copy is specifically discussing the technology platform.
- Write the full organization names on first reference—**American Association
  of Neurological Surgeons (AANS)** and **American Society for Radiation
  Oncology (ASTRO)**—then use the acronyms. Do not add trademark symbols to the
  organization names without specific current brand guidance.
- Do not reproduce company or society logos, branded graphics, screenshots, or
  trade dress without permission. Text-only names and links are sufficient for
  this portfolio.
- Link to authoritative public pages rather than reproducing proprietary
  product descriptions or marketing materials.
- Recheck current brand guidance immediately before publication because
  trademark ownership, corporate entities, and preferred attribution language
  can change.

#### Proposed site-wide attribution

> VoyantFlow™, Quentry®, Brainlab®, Stryker®, TraumaCad®, OfficePACS, CasePlan,
> and other product or organization names are trademarks or registered
> trademarks of their respective owners. Their use on this site is for
> identification only and does not imply endorsement, sponsorship, or current
> affiliation.

This attribution can appear in small text on the future Work page or in a site
legal/footer area. It does not need to interrupt each project entry.
